Design Philosophy

Cards give you options.
Knowledge scores the point.

Most gacha games are won by whoever spends the most. Kacha breaks that link — a whale who owns everything but knows nothing still loses to a genuine superfan.

💸

Skill Over Wallet

Spending accelerates collection and unlocks strategic options. It never buys a faster correct answer. The song either sounds familiar — or it doesn't.

🎵

Collection is Study

Every card lets you hear its era's clips. Building your deck is also learning the catalog — new fans grow their knowledge and their collection together.

⚖️

Fairness is Structural

The battle format itself prevents cheating, answer disputes, and newbie walls. No text box means no Shazam, no title-spelling arguments, no ambiguity.

Setlist Clash

A music duel built to be impossible to cheat.

Instead of typing a song title, you tap the card you think the clip belongs to. The board is your answer space — there's nothing to identify that isn't already in front of you.

1

Build Your Lineup

Pick 5 cards from your collection. Each card brings its era's audio pool into the match, steering clips toward groups you know cold.

2

A 2–3 Second Clip Plays

The server picks a card from the shared 10-card board and fires a short clip. Both players hear it simultaneously, with server-authoritative timing.

3

Tap the Right Card

Race to tap the matching card. First correct tap scores. A wrong tap locks you out for the round — mashing gets punished, knowledge gets rewarded.

4

First to 5 Wins

Quick, high-energy sessions. The trailing player's ability resources charge faster, keeping every match tense until the final round.

Card Abilities

5 abilities. Infinite strategy.

Rarity grants access to stronger ability options — not faster correct answers. Deck-building is about knowing which abilities complement what you know.

⚓
Anchor
Takes two correct IDs to knock out. Tank card for a group you know cold — your opponent needs two right answers to clear it.
⚡
Bias Boost
Gives you a head start when this card's clip plays. Rewards fielding groups you genuinely know best — stack it with your main bias.
🎭
Decoy
Seeds a sound-alike clip to bait wrong taps from your opponent. High-risk, high-reward — a baited lockout is a free point.
🔦
Spotlight
Clips play longer — easier for both players — but score double if you claim them. A calculated gamble: easier to hear, bigger payoff.
🎤
Encore
Charges faster when you're behind. The comeback lever that keeps a 0–4 deficit tense without rewarding losing outright.

Daily Quests

Steady coin faucet through daily challenges, collection milestones, and in-match feats. Something to do every session, even without PvP matches.

🤖

Tour Mode (PvE)

Solo stages against AI with difficulty that ramps one axis at a time — shorter clips, then B-sides, then audio manipulation. Safe onboarding that never stomps newcomers.

🏆

Seasonal Resets

Seasons reset rank with exclusive rewards for your peak tier. Fresh start for everyone, but your collection and knowledge carry over permanently.

⚡ Wager Mode

Optional high-stakes matches where players ante coins or shards. The loser forfeits the pot. Genuine stakes without ever putting your cards at risk — no whale-stomping, no real-money vector. Cards are never on the line; only the pot is.

Fandom at the Center

Guilds are fandoms.
Rivalry is the point.

Bias declaration, fandom clubs, and group-versus-group events make Setlist Clash native fandom competition — not a generic card game clan system.

Join a fan club, recruit members, and compete as a unit. Guilds are organized by group allegiance, not just rank brackets.

Group vs. Group Events

Weekly leaderboard wars between rival fandoms. Every match your guild wins contributes to the group score — fandom pride on the line.

👤

Fandom Profile

Showcase your bias, rank, collection highlights, and win rate. A public card that broadcasts your fandom identity.
